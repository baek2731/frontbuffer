Switching Android phones means your Samsung Health data needs to move with you. The good news: Samsung Health backs up automatically through Samsung Cloud. The catch: it only works if sync is active on your old device before you make the switch. Here is exactly what transfers, what does not, and what to do when things go wrong.

## How Samsung Health Data Actually Transfers

Samsung Health uses Samsung Cloud for automatic synchronization. When you sign into the same Samsung account on a new device, your health history restores automatically. This covers step counts, activity records, sleep data, heart rate measurements, and workout logs.

This is the only method that actually restores data into the app. Everything else — manual exports, CSV files — creates a personal archive you can read but cannot re-import.

**Before you switch, verify sync is active:**

1. Open Samsung Health on your current phone
2. Tap the profile icon or three-dot menu → Settings
3. Find "Sync with Samsung Cloud" and confirm it is enabled
4. Wait for a full sync to complete before setting up your new device

If sync was never enabled, your data has not been backed up to the cloud. In that case, your only option is a manual export for archiving — the data will not appear in the app on your new phone.

## Manual Export — What It Is and What It Is Not

Samsung Health lets you export your data as CSV, HTML, or PDF files. To access it:

1. Open Samsung Health → Profile icon or three-dot menu → Settings
2. Scroll to "Download personal data" or "Export data"
3. Select data types and format → Export

This is useful for keeping a personal record or sharing specific metrics with a healthcare provider. It is not a backup solution. Exported files cannot be re-imported into Samsung Health. If you export and then switch phones without cloud sync enabled, the app on your new device starts fresh.

CSV exports contain detailed raw data — heart rate readings, step counts by hour — but the format uses abbreviations that require some effort to interpret. Most users find HTML or PDF more readable for a quick reference.

## Common Issues and How to Fix Them

**Sync shows as active but data is missing after switching**
Give the app time. Large health libraries can take 30–60 minutes to fully restore after signing in on a new device. Check back before assuming the data is gone.

**Export process stalls or shows a server error**
This happens occasionally. Try again with a stable Wi-Fi connection. Do not attempt exports on mobile data for large libraries.

**New device shows a clean slate despite cloud sync being enabled**
Confirm you are using the exact same Samsung account email on both devices. A secondary or backup account will not have the data.

## Step-by-Step: Switching Phones Without Losing Data

1. On your old phone, open Samsung Health → Settings → enable "Sync with Samsung Cloud"
2. Let it sync fully — check the last sync timestamp in settings
3. Optional: export your data as CSV or PDF for a personal archive
4. Set up your new Android phone and sign in with the same Samsung account
5. Install Samsung Health and sign in
6. Wait for data to restore — check activity history to confirm

If you are switching from Samsung to a non-Samsung Android phone, Samsung Health data does not transfer to Google Fit or Health Connect automatically. You would need to export manually and use a third-party conversion tool, or accept that the history stays in Samsung Health on your old device.
